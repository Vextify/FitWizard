    What is BMI?


    BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a numerical value that represents the relationship between a person's weight and height. It is a commonly used tool to assess whether an individual's weight falls within a healthy range. BMI provides a general indication of body composition and can help identify potential health risks associated with weight. The calculation involves dividing an individual's weight (in kilograms) by their height (in meters) squared. The resulting BMI value is then categorized into different ranges, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.
